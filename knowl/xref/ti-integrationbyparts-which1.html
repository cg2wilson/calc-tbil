Activity 7.1.4.

Consider the integral \(\ds \int x^9\ln(x) \,dx\text{.}\) Suppose we proceed using integration by parts. We choose \(u=\ln(x)\) and \(dv=x^9\,dx\text{.}\)
(a)
What is \(du\text{?}\)
(b)
What is \(v\text{?}\)
(c)
What do you get when plugging these pieces into integration by parts?
(d)
Does the new integral \(\ds \int v\,du\) seem easier or harder to compute than the original integral \(\ds \int x^9\ln(x) \,dx\text{?}\)
  1. The original integral is easier to compute.
  2. The new integral is easier to compute.
  3. Neither integral seems harder than the other one.
