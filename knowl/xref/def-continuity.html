Definition 1.4.4. Continuity.

A function \(f\) is continuous at \(x = a\) provided that
  1. \(f\) has a limit as \(x \to a\)
  2. \(f\) is defined at \(x = a\) (equivalently, \(a\) is in the domain of \(f\)), and
  3. \(\ds\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)\text{.}\)
A function is continuous on the interval \(I\) if it is continuous at all inputs in \(I\text{.}\)
If a function is not continuous at \(x=a\) or on \(I\text{,}\) it is called discontinuous at that input or on \(I\text{.}\)
