Again, consider \(f(x) = x^2\) on the interval \([0,2]\text{.}\) We will compute \(L_8\) and \(R_8\text{.}\)
(a)
Which is the appropriate expression for \(L_8\text{?}\)
  1. \(\displaystyle L_8 = \sum_{i=0}^8 \lrpar{(x_i)^2\cdot \dfrac{1}{4}}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle L_8 = \sum_{i=0}^7 \lrpar{(x_i)^2\cdot \dfrac{1}{4}}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle L_8 = \sum_{i=1}^8 \lrpar{(x_i)^2\cdot \dfrac{1}{4}}\)
(b)
The value of \(x_0\) is \(0\text{,}\) because that is the beginning of the interval; what is the value of \(x_6\text{?}\) Use this idea to find \(x_0,x_1,...,x_8\)
  1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{3}{4}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{6}{4}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{1}{2}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{6}{8}\)
