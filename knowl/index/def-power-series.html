Definition 9.1.1. Power Series.

A series of the form
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty c_nx^n = c_0 + c_1x + c_2x^2 + \cdots \end{equation*}
where \(x\) is a variable and the coefficients \(c_n\) are constants, is called a power series centered at \(x= 0\).
A power series centered at \(x = a\) is a power series of the form
\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty c_n(x-a)^n = c_0 + c_1(x-a) + c_2(x-a)^2 + \cdots \end{equation*}
A power series is said to converge on the interval \(I\) if the series converges on \(I\text{.}\)
