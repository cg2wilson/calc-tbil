Definition 13.3.2. Surface Integral (Scalar).

Given a parametrization \(\textbf{r}(u,v) = \lrangle{f(u,v),g(u,v),h(u,v)}\) of the surface \(S\text{,}\) the surface integral of a scalar function \(f(x,y,z)\) over \(S\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \iint_S f(x,y,z)\, dS = \iint_R f(\textbf{r}(u,v))|\textbf{r}_u\times\textbf{r}_v|\, du\, dv \end{equation*}
Here, the term \(dS\) is the surface area differential
