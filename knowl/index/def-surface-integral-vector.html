Definition 13.3.5. Surface Integral (Vector).

Let \(\textbf{F}\) be a continuous vector field with a domain that contains the oriented surface \(S\) with unit normal vector \(\textbf{N}\text{.}\) The surface integral of \(\textbf{F}\) over \(S\) is given by
\begin{equation*} \iint_S \textbf{F}\cdot d\textbf{S} = \iint_S \textbf{F}\cdot \textbf{N}\, dS \end{equation*}
where \(\textbf{N} = \dfrac{\textbf{r}_u\times \textbf{r}_v}{|\textbf{r}_u\times \textbf{r}_v|}\) for some parametrization \(\textbf{r}(u,v)\) of \(S\text{.}\)
A computational form is given by
\begin{equation*} \iint_S \textbf{F}\cdot \textbf{N}\, dS = \iint_D \textbf{F}(\textbf{r}(u,v))\cdot (\textbf{r}_u\times \textbf{r}_v)\, dA \end{equation*}
