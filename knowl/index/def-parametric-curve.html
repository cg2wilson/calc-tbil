Definition 12.1.1. Parametric Curve.

If \(x\) and \(y\) are continuous functions of the variable \(t\) on some interval \(I\text{,}\) then the equations \(x = x(t)\) and \(y = y(t)\) are called parametric equations and \(t\) is called the parameter. The graph of parametric equations- the set of points \((x,y)\) obtained as \(t\) varies over \(I\)- is called a parametric curve.
