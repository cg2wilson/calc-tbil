Definition 11.1.4. Type I and Type II Regions.

A region \(D\) is called a type I region if it lies between two vertical lines and the graphs of two continuous functions \(g_1(x)\) and \(g_2(x)\text{,}\) i.e.
\begin{equation*} D = \lrbrace{(x,y)\in R^2\mid a\leq x\leq b, g_1(x)\leq y\leq g_2(x)} \end{equation*}
A region \(D\) is called a type II region if it lies between two horizontal lines and the graphs of two continuous functions \(h_1(y)\) and \(h_2(y)\text{,}\) i.e.
\begin{equation*} D = \lrbrace{(x,y)\in\R^2\mid h_1(y)\leq x \leq h_2(y), c\leq y \leq d} \end{equation*}
