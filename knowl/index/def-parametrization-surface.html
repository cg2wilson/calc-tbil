Definition 13.2.2. Parametrization of a Surface.

Suppose that the continuous vector function
\begin{equation*} \textbf{r}(u,v) = \lrangle{f(u,v),g(u,v),h(u,v)} \end{equation*}
is defined on some region \(R\) in the \(uv\)-plane and one-to-one on the interior of \(R\text{.}\) The range of \(\textbf{r}\) is called a surface \(S\) defined or traced by \(\textbf{r}\text{.}\) This vector function along with the domain \(R\) is called a parametrization of the surface \(S\text{,}\) the variables \(u,v\) are called parameters, and \(R\) is called the parameter domain.
