Definition 6.2.2. Washer Method.

Let \(f\) and \(g\) be continuous functions on \([a,b]\) such that \(f(x) \geq g(x)\) on \([a,b]\text{.}\) Then, the volume of the oslid created by rotating the region between \(f\) and \(g\) about the \(x-\)axis is given by
\begin{equation*} V = \ds \int_a^b \pi (R(x))^2\, dx - \int_a^b \pi(r(x))^2\, dx \end{equation*}
where \(R(x)\) is the radius associated with \(f(x)\) and \(r(x)\) is the radius associated with \(g(x)\)
