Theorem 7.1.1. Integration by Parts.

By the product rule, \(\dfrac{d}{dx}[uv]=u'v+uv'\) and, subsequently, \(uv'=\dfrac{d}{dx}[uv]-u'v\text{.}\) There is a dual integration technique reversing this process, known as integration by parts.
This technique involves using algebra to rewrite an integral of a product of functions in the form \(\ds \int (u)\,dv\) and then using the equality
\begin{equation*} \ds \int (u)\,dv=uv-\int(v)\,du\text{.} \end{equation*}
