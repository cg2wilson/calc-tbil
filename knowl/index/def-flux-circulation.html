Definition 12.3.7. Flux and Circulation.

The flux of a vector field \(\textbf{F}\) across a curve \(C\) is given by the integral
\begin{equation*} \ds \int_C \textbf{F}\cdot \textbf{N}\, ds = \int_a^b \textbf{F}(\textbf{r}(t))\cdot \textbf{n}(t)\, dt \end{equation*}
where \(\textbf{r}(t) = \lrangle{x(t),y(t)}\) and \(\textbf{n}(t) = \lrangle{y'(t),-x'(t)}\)
Let \(C\) be an oriented, closed curve. Then, the circulation of the vector field \(\textbf{F}\) along \(C\) is defined to be the line integral along \(C\text{,}\) but is denoted as
\begin{equation*} \oint_C \textbf{F}\cdot \textbf{T}\, ds \end{equation*}
