Definition 10.4.3. Directional Derivative.

Let \(f(x,y)\) be a differentiable function of two variables, and let \(\textbf{u} = \lrangle{a,b}\) be a unit vector. The directional derivative of \(f(x,y)\) in the direction of \(\textbf{u}\) is given by
\begin{equation*} D_{\textbf{u}}(f) = \nabla f\cdot\textbf{u}= f_x(x,y)a + f_y(x,y)b \end{equation*}
or
\begin{equation*} \lim_{h\to 0} \dfrac{f(x+ah,y+bh)-f(x,y)}{h} \end{equation*}
